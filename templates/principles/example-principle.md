# Principle: AI writing must carry zero telltales

> **For future Claude:** An operating principle — how the owner ACTS, not just what they know. Principles are the output of the judgment layer: a source is run through `self/thinking-lens.md` and what comes out is a first-person prescription. This example was derived from an article that only *described* AI-bot telltales in LinkedIn comments; the prescription is the owner's.

**Trigger.** When I publish or send anything AI-assisted in my name — posts, comments, articles, outreach, docs.

**Rule.** No AI telltales survive to the final version. If a pattern reads as machine-generated (stock AI phrasings, formulaic structure, generic enthusiasm), it gets rewritten in my voice before it ships. Using AI for speed is fine; letting its fingerprints through is not.

**Why.** Telltales are disrespectful to the reader, they make the writing sloppy, and they are dishonest about the care that went into it. My public credibility is the asset; machine-voice content spends it.

**Provenance.** Article ingested 2026-07-02 describing AI bots commenting on LinkedIn posts with easily spotted telltales (the source described the phenomenon; the prescription is the owner's judgment, session-example).
